# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, remove team impediments, and coach the team on agile practices. They foster continuous improvement and ensure the team operates efficiently within the chosen agile framework.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, reviews, and retrospectives
- Remove blockers and impediments to team progress
- Coach team members on agile principles and practices
- Shield the team from distractions and external pressures
- Track team metrics (velocity, burndown, cycle time) to identify improvement areas
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement and psychological safety

### Goals
- Maximize team productivity and flow
- Reduce cycle time and eliminate waste
- Build high-performing, self-organizing teams
- Ensure sustainable pace and prevent burnout

### Typical Communication
- Daily facilitation of standups and team coordination
- Weekly retrospective facilitation and action tracking
- Regular coaching conversations with team members
- Coordination with Project Manager on delivery commitments

### Interactions with other roles
- **Project Manager**: Collaborates on timeline commitments, escalates systemic blockers
- **Product Manager**: Ensures backlog readiness and clarity for team planning
- **Developers**: Coaches on agile practices, facilitates collaboration, removes impediments
- **QA/Testing**: Coordinates testing integration into sprint workflow

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers conduct user research, create wireframes and prototypes, and validate usability. They ensure products are intuitive, accessible, and meet user needs through iterative design and testing.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Define information architecture and user flows
- Collaborate with PM and developers on design feasibility
- Validate designs through user feedback and testing
- Maintain design systems and component libraries

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, user-centered designs
- Reduce friction in user workflows
- Ensure accessibility and inclusive design
- Improve user satisfaction and engagement metrics

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critiques with stakeholders
- User research findings presentations
- Iterative feedback sessions during development
- Participation in sprint demos to validate implementation

### Interactions with other roles
- **Product Manager**: Partners on user needs, validates solutions, prioritizes UX debt
- **Developers**: Collaborates on implementation feasibility, provides design specs and assets
- **Project Manager**: Communicates design timeline and dependencies
- **Customer Success**: Gathers user feedback and validates design decisions

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers coordinate release timelines, ensure deployment readiness, and manage the end-to-end release process. They bridge development and operations to ensure smooth, reliable releases.

### Responsibilities
- Coordinate release schedules and deployment windows
- Ensure all pre-release requirements are met (tests, approvals, documentation)
- Manage release pipeline and deployment automation
- Create and maintain deployment runbooks and rollback plans
- Communicate release status to stakeholders
- Conduct post-release verification and monitor for issues

### Goals
- Ensure reliable, low-risk deployments
- Minimize deployment-related incidents
- Reduce time from code complete to production
- Maintain clear release communication and documentation

### Typical Communication
- Release planning meetings with PM and engineering
- Pre-release readiness reviews and go/no-go decisions
- Release notifications to stakeholders and support teams
- Post-release status updates and incident coordination

### Interactions with other roles
- **Project Manager**: Aligns release timeline with project milestones
- **Developers**: Coordinates code freeze, merge schedule, and deployment timing
- **QA/Testing**: Validates test completion and sign-off before release
- **Technical Writer**: Ensures release notes and documentation are ready
- **Customer Success**: Coordinates customer communication and support readiness

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers own end-user documentation, internal guides, release notes, and process documentation. They ensure documentation is accurate, accessible, and maintained throughout the product lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain user-facing documentation
- Write release notes and changelog entries
- Document internal processes and runbooks
- Collaborate with engineers to ensure technical accuracy
- Maintain documentation site and information architecture
- Review PRs for documentation completeness

### Goals
- Reduce support burden through clear documentation
- Ensure documentation stays current with product changes
- Improve discoverability and usability of documentation
- Enable self-service for users and internal teams

### Typical Communication
- Documentation reviews with subject matter experts
- Regular check-ins with PM and developers on upcoming changes
- Release notes coordination with Release Manager
- Feedback collection from users and support teams

### Interactions with other roles
- **Project Manager**: Aligns documentation timeline with release schedule
- **Product Manager**: Ensures documentation reflects product vision and user needs
- **Developers**: Collaborates on technical accuracy and API documentation
- **Release Manager**: Coordinates release notes and deployment documentation
- **Customer Success**: Incorporates customer feedback into documentation improvements

---

## Customer Success

### Role Summary
Customer Success representatives serve as the voice of the customer within the organization. They gather feedback, ensure customer satisfaction, and escalate product issues to drive continuous improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Build relationships with customers and understand their needs
- Gather and synthesize customer feedback on features and usability
- Track customer health metrics and identify at-risk accounts
- Escalate product issues and feature requests to PM and engineering
- Facilitate customer onboarding and training
- Communicate product updates and changes to customers

### Goals
- Maximize customer satisfaction and retention
- Reduce churn and increase product adoption
- Ensure customer voice influences product decisions
- Enable customers to achieve their desired outcomes

### Typical Communication
- Regular customer check-ins and business reviews
- Internal feedback sessions with PM and product team
- Escalation reports for urgent customer issues
- Customer training and webinar sessions

### Interactions with other roles
- **Product Manager**: Provides customer insights, validates feature priorities, reports impact
- **Project Manager**: Communicates customer expectations and urgency
- **Developers**: Escalates customer-impacting bugs and technical issues
- **Technical Writer**: Provides customer perspective on documentation gaps
- **Release Manager**: Coordinates customer communication for releases and incidents
